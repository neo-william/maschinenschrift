The registry gathers works that have declared their own origin. Admission happens through self-declaration. There is no fee, no editorial verdict, no review board.

What is checked is the consistency of the statement — whether the declaration fits the work, whether the work is bibliographically findable — not the truth of the self-disclosure. That rests with the author. The registry is signal, not gate: it controls no market, but records those who have openly stated how their texts came into being.

Whoever is registered has set the seal and stands publicly by it. Registration is a [self-declaration](/submit/), not a certificate.
